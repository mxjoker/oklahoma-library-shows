## What Happens (narrative)
Walk through:
1. Intro: Joe shows kids a trick — then reveals (loosely) that they're going to learn one
2. Teach trick #1: Simple card trick or coin trick — everyone follows along
3. Practice time: Kids practice on each other — laughter, mishaps, victories
4. Level up: Joe shows a harder trick as a challenge/demo
5. Performance: Volunteers perform their trick for the group — confidence spotlight moment
6. Takeaway: Kids leave knowing 1–2 tricks they can actually do at home

Key point: this is PARTICIPATORY, not a spectator show. Every child is active.

## Educational Connections
**CSLP tie-in:** "Unearth a Story" — learning magic is like learning to tell a story with your hands.
**Learning outcomes (from ODL):**
- Builds fine motor skills, memory, and creative problem-solving
- Develops public speaking and performance confidence
- Encourages critical thinking, communication, and creativity
- Reinforces focus and sequencing

**Why this fits a library:** Libraries build readers and thinkers. Magic builds focus, sequencing, patience, and the willingness to try again after failing — all literacy-adjacent skills.
