# 2. How to Book a Performer

**File:** `resources/how-to-book-a-performer.html`
**URL:** `/resources/how-to-book-a-performer`
**Gate:** Free — no email required

## SEO
- **Primary keyword:** `how to book a performer for your library`
- **Secondary:** `library entertainer booking process`, `summer reading program performer checklist`
- **Title tag:** `How to Book a Performer for Your Library | Oklahoma Library Shows`
- **Meta description:** `A step-by-step guide for librarians on finding, vetting, and booking a summer reading program performer. Free download — no email required.`

## Content Outline
1. **Start early** — timelines for booking (6–12 months in advance ideal)
2. **Define your needs** — audience size, age range, indoor/outdoor, educational goals
3. **Vet your options** — what to look for (CSLP alignment, library-specific experience, transparent pricing, references)
4. **Get budget approval** — how to make the case to your director; what documentation helps
5. **The booking conversation** — what to ask a performer before committing
6. **Logistics checklist** — setup time, space requirements, equipment, what the library provides
7. **After the show** — how to collect feedback and build the case for next year

At the end: "If you're looking for a performer in Oklahoma or nearby states — we can help."
→ CTA to booking modal

## Format
Long-form article with headers. Aim for 800–1,200 words.
Can be printed as a checklist PDF (add a "Download as PDF" button if Joe wants to make one).

## SEO
- **Primary keyword:** `summer reading program planning guide librarians`

- **Title tag:** `Summer Reading Program Planning Guide for Librarians | Oklahoma Library Shows`
- **Meta description:** `The complete guide to planning a summer reading program — from CSLP theme to performer booking. Free for Oklahoma librarians with email signup.`

## Content Outline
1. **Understanding the CSLP calendar** — how themes are announced, how to plan around them
2. **Timeline: 12 months before summer** — what to lock in now
3. **Budgeting for performers** — typical costs, how to justify in the budget
4. **Finding CSLP-aligned programs** — what to look for, where to look (ODL catalog, ALA, local)
5. **Booking checklist** — everything to confirm before finalizing a performer
6. **Promoting your summer program** — channel ideas for library communications
7. **Measuring success** — attendance, patron feedback, what to report to directors
8. **2027 planning note** — "Mysteries Await at Your Library" is the 2027 theme; here's what to start planning now
