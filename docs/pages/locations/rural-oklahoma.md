## Why This Page Exists (Strategic Note)
Rural Oklahoma libraries are:
1. Underserved by performers (most won't travel there)
2. Underserved in search — no one is targeting "rural Oklahoma library shows"
3. High loyalty customers — if Joe shows up and does great work, they book every year
4. **Mileage clustering is the key value prop here:** rural libraries that coordinate booking
   dates with neighboring libraries can share the travel cost, making it far more affordable

This page exists to say: "We will come to you. Here's how to make it affordable."

## The Clustering Pitch (main value prop section)
This should be a dedicated visual callout on the page:

> **How clustering saves rural libraries money**
>
> Library travel fees are based on distance. But when two or three nearby libraries book
> shows on the same trip, the travel cost is divided across all of them — meaning each
> library pays significantly less.
>
> **Example:** Three libraries within 45 minutes of each other book shows on consecutive
> days. Instead of each paying a full travel fee from OKC (2.5 hours away), the total
> travel is split three ways — each library's effective cost drops.
>
> **How to do it:** Call your neighboring library directors before you submit your inquiry.
> Tell us you're coordinating and we'll build the route together.
>
